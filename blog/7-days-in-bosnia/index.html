7 days in Bosnia and Herzegovina: a first-timer's route

Published May 28, 2026 By Balkan Local

Bosnia and Herzegovina rewards a slow week. The distances are short, the coffee is slower than you expect, and the best moments tend to happen between the famous sights rather than at them. This is a gentle first-timer’s route: enough structure to feel sorted, enough slack to follow a recommendation down a side street.

Trip at a glance

Length
7 days, one-way Sarajevo to Mostar
Best months
Late May, June, and September
Getting around
A rental car is easiest; intercity buses are good and cheap
Currency
Convertible mark (BAM), pegged to the euro

The route, day by day

  1. 1

    Day 1 and 2: Sarajevo

    Bascarsija, the coppersmiths, the Tunnel of Hope, and an evening on the hills above the city. Build in a slow morning coffee; it is the whole point.

  2. 2

    Day 3: Jajce

    A waterfall in the middle of town and a fortress above it. An easy half-day stop on the way west.

  3. 3

    Day 4: Travnik and Pliva lakes

    Vizier-era old town, then the watermills on the lakes for lunch with your feet near the water.

  4. 4

    Day 5: Mostar

    Arrive before the day-trippers leave. The Stari Most is worth the crowds, but the side lanes are where you will want to linger.

  5. 5

    Day 6: Blagaj and Pocitelj

    The dervish house at the spring of the Buna, then the stone-stepped Ottoman village downriver.

  6. 6

    Day 7: Kravice waterfalls

    A wide curtain of falls and pools to swim in before the trip home.

A local tip on coffee

Bosnian coffee is a ritual, not a transaction. You will be served a small copper pot, a cup, sugar cubes, and usually a piece of Turkish delight. Pour slowly, sip slowly, and do not rush to leave. Cafes do not mind.
