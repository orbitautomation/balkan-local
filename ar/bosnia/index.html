Live it like a local

The Bosnia you only find by slowing down.

The country reveals itself in its small rituals. These are the moments we steer you toward, the ones that turn a trip into a memory.

Coffee, slowly

Bosnian coffee is not a caffeine run, it is an hour. Sit with a fildžan and a cigarette on a terrace above the rooftops and let the afternoon go nowhere. Locals call it ćejf, the art of quiet pleasure.

The evening korzo

At dusk, whole towns drift out to walk the main street, greet half the people they know and end up over a drink. Join the promenade in Sarajevo or Mostar and you stop being a tourist.

River and mountain time

A swim in a cold green river, a plate of fresh trout, a coffee made on a wood stove in a highland village. The best afternoons here have no itinerary at all.

Good to know

Bosnia, in practical terms.

The basics worth knowing before you go. We handle the rest.

Currency

Convertible mark (KM / BAM), pegged near 1.95 to the euro. Cash is king in small towns.

Language

Bosnian, Croatian and Serbian. English is widely understood in cities and by younger people.

Best time to visit

Late spring to early autumn for warmth and open mountain roads. Sarajevo is magic under snow too.

Entry

Many nationalities, including EU, UK, US and GCC passports, enter visa free for up to 90 days. Check your own passport before you travel.

Getting around

Distances are short but roads wind through mountains. We arrange private drivers so you never wait at a bus stop.

Tipping

Cards work in cities, less so in villages. Rounding up the bill is normal and appreciated.
