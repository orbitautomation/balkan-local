Frequently asked questions.

Honest answers to the questions first-time visitors ask most. Still unsure about something? We are always happy to help.

Is Bosnia safe to visit?

Yes. Bosnia is one of Europe's safer destinations, with low violent crime and a genuinely warm welcome for visitors. Use the usual common sense in busy areas, and stick to marked paths in remote countryside. Every business we list is independently verified, so you always know who you are dealing with.

Do I need a visa to visit Bosnia?

Citizens of the EU, UK, US, Canada, Australia, the GCC states and many other countries can enter visa-free for up to 90 days. Entry rules can change, so confirm the current requirement for your nationality before you book.

When is the best time to visit Bosnia?

Spring and autumn are mild, green and quiet; summer is warm with long days and a cool escape from the Gulf heat; winter brings affordable skiing on the 1984 Olympic mountains. Our travel guide breaks down each season in more detail.

How do you verify the businesses you list?

Before any partner appears, we check registration, licensing, ownership and insurance, review their real-world reputation, and assess their standards and pricing. A single substantiated complaint is enough for us to remove a partner from the platform.

Is Bosnia a good destination for Muslim travelers?

Very much so. Close to half the country is Muslim, so halal food and mosques are part of everyday life. We mark halal and family-friendly places clearly, and show live prayer times and the nearest mosques throughout your trip.

What currency is used, and can I pay by card?

The currency is the Convertible Mark (KM or BAM), pegged to the euro at a fixed rate. Cards are widely accepted in cities and hotels, but Bosnia is still a cash-friendly country, so carry some cash for taxis, markets and smaller towns.

Will I be able to get by in English?

Yes. English is widely used across tourism, and our guides and traveler support work in English, German and Arabic. The local languages are Bosnian, Croatian and Serbian.

Can I book a trip now, and is there an app?

We are building the full booking app now. In the meantime, join the early-access list and we will help you plan directly. Everything you see on the site reflects how Balkan Local works once you are booking with us.
